**AI Builders Digest — May 1, 2026**

Yesterday we saw Vercel betting on agent-first developer tools. Today, we're seeing what happens when that bet pays off: companies are creating entirely new job roles just to manage AI systems, and the biggest names in tech are racing to put their most powerful models directly into business workflows.

**Box CEO Aaron Levie is hiring "agent engineers" for internal business processes**
Box CEO Aaron Levie announced his company is creating new "agent engineering" roles focused on getting AI agents working effectively on critical internal business processes. These roles look like internal full-stack engineers whose job is to wire up company systems and build secure, governed agents for workflows. Levie expects this type of role to become standard across companies.
Why it matters: This is the clearest signal yet that AI agents are moving from experimental to essential. When a Fortune 500 CEO starts hiring full-time staff just to manage AI systems, every company will need these roles within 18 months.

**OpenAI releases GPT-5.5-Cyber exclusively for cybersecurity defenders**
OpenAI CEO Sam Altman announced the company is rolling out GPT-5.5-Cyber, a specialized cybersecurity model, to critical cyber defenders over the next few days. The company plans to work with the government and private sector to establish trusted access protocols for cybersecurity applications, with the goal of rapidly securing company infrastructure.
Why it matters: OpenAI just admitted that cybersecurity is too important to leave to general-purpose AI models. If you're responsible for your company's security infrastructure, you're about to get access to AI capabilities that didn't exist last week.
https://x.com/sama/status/2049712078836170843

**Google's Gemini can now generate and export files in any format you need**
Google product manager Josh Woodward announced Gemini can now create files and export them directly to Google Docs, Word, PDFs, Excel, Google Sheets, CSV, Google Slides, Markdown, LaTeX, TXT, and RTF formats. Users simply tell Gemini what they want to create and specify the format. The feature is available globally across all Gemini surfaces.
Why it matters: This kills the "AI can generate content but then you have to copy-paste it everywhere" problem. When your AI can deliver finished documents directly to your workflow, the friction of using AI for business tasks just disappeared.
https://x.com/joshwoodward/status/2049524400131838436

**Cursor's Ryo Lu: you can now build agent systems with the same tools powering Cursor**
Cursor engineer Ryo Lu shared that developers can now build their own agent systems using Cursor's multi-model harness, which works on both local and cloud infrastructure. This gives developers access to the same underlying technology that powers Cursor's AI coding capabilities.
https://x.com/ryolu_/status/2049529609725804575

**Latent Space reports coding agents are "breaking containment" across industries**
The Latent Space newsletter highlighted major updates from both Codex and Claude, with Codex launching "Codex for Work" to expand beyond coding into general knowledge work, and Claude adding support for creative tools like Blender, Adobe Creative Cloud, and Ableton. The newsletter describes this as coding agents expanding into every type of computer work.
Why it matters: The AI tools built for programmers turned out to be the best AI tools for everyone. Your marketing team is about to start using the same AI that writes code to create presentations, edit videos, and manage projects.
