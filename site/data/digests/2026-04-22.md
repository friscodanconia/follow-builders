**Vercel CEO clarifies security incident details**
Vercel CEO Guillermo Rauch posted an update addressing ongoing questions about the company's security breach. He emphasized that the official security bulletin is the authoritative source and clarified a key misconception: deleting compromised credentials isn't the same as rotating them. The company has directly contacted all customers they believe were impacted.
Why it matters: This is what transparency looks like during a security incident. Other AI platform companies should take notes on how to communicate with customers when things go wrong.

**Product manager discovers AI agents' secret strength: HTML**
Zara Zhang observed that AI agents produce much better results when working in HTML rather than XML, suggesting that agents "speak HTML as their native language." She noted this applies to everything from slides to other design outputs where agents can express themselves more naturally in HTML format.
Why it matters: Stop fighting your AI tools and start working with their strengths. If you're still asking agents to output XML or custom formats, you're making them work harder than they need to.
https://x.com/zarazhangrui/status/2046454622852657264

**Design agents might be more fun than coding agents**
Product manager Peter Yang shared his enthusiasm for using AI agents for design work, claiming it's "more fun than coding with agents." The comment suggests he's found design tasks particularly well-suited to AI assistance.
Why it matters: While everyone focuses on AI replacing developers, the creative collaboration between humans and AI in design work might be where the real magic happens first.
