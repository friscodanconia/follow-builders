**Box CEO Aaron Levie: AI will create security jobs, not kill them**
Levie predicts that security is about to have its "Jevons paradox moment" where better AI tools actually increase demand for security talent rather than reduce it. His reasoning: AI will generate 100x more code, and autonomous security tools can find vulnerabilities faster but can't handle the response. More real findings surfaced faster means more triage, more remediation, and more architectural decisions that need human judgment.
Why it matters: Every company planning to cut security headcount because "AI will automate it" has this backwards. When AI agents start writing most of your code, you'll need more security people, not fewer.

**Vercel CEO Guillermo Rauch's customer feedback strategy**
Rauch shared his approach for getting brutal, useful feedback: create a group chat with your most demanding customers, put your engineering leads in it, and "face the music." He's running one for v0 and actively recruiting committed users who will share excellent feedback.
Why it matters: Most product teams get feedback through surveys and support tickets — sanitized and delayed. Putting engineers directly in a chat with power users who aren't afraid to complain is uncomfortable but fast.
