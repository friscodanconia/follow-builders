**Google's TPU v8 targets the "agentic era"**
Google announced the eighth generation of its TPU chips, featuring two specialized processors designed specifically for AI agents and autonomous systems. The company is positioning these chips as purpose-built for the next wave of AI applications that go beyond simple text generation.
Why it matters: Google is betting hardware that AI agents will be everywhere soon. If they're right, every cloud provider without agent-optimized chips just fell behind.

**Microsoft's AutoAdapt solves the "how do I actually deploy this AI model?" problem**
Microsoft Research released AutoAdapt, a framework that automatically figures out how to adapt large language models for specific industries like healthcare, law, or incident response. Instead of manually choosing between approaches like RAG or fine-tuning, AutoAdapt uses an "agentic planner" to map out the entire adaptation process and optimize within budget and latency constraints. The system turns what used to be weeks of trial-and-error into automated pipelines.
Why it matters: Every company trying to deploy AI in production hits the same wall: general models don't work for specialized tasks, and fixing them is expensive guesswork. Microsoft just automated the guesswork.
https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/research/blog/autoadapt-automated-domain-adaptation-for-large-language-models/
