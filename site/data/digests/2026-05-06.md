**Y Combinator CEO Garry Tan's all-in-one AI setup**
Y Combinator CEO Garry Tan explained what makes his AI tool gbrain different from everything else on the market. It's not just memory, coding help, or search — it combines all three into one unified system with a single query interface. Tan uses it daily with his 100,000 markdown file setup alongside OpenClaw and Hermes agents.
Why it matters: The head of the world's most famous startup accelerator isn't using five different AI tools. He's using one tool that does five things well. That's the direction the whole market is heading.

**The three waves of AI that matter, according to Peter Yang**
Product advisor Peter Yang outlined the progression he sees in AI adoption: coding tools came first, knowledge work is happening now, and personal agents are the final frontier. It's a simple framework that cuts through the noise about what AI can actually do today versus what's still coming.
Why it matters: If you're wondering whether your job is next, this gives you a timeline. Developers already live with AI coding assistants. Office workers are starting to use AI for research and writing. Everyone else has time to figure this out.

**Crabbox ships remote development environments that actually work**
Peter Steinberger released Crabbox 0.5.0, bringing desktop and browser-based development environments to the cloud. The update adds VNC support, authenticated web access, Windows instances with WSL2, and screenshot capabilities. It's positioned as "remote CI boxes, now suspiciously usable."
Why it matters: Every company trying to give AI agents access to real development environments needs something like this. When your AI needs to test code on Windows, Mac, and Linux, spinning up containers isn't enough.
