**AI Builders Digest — April 26, 2026**

Box CEO Aaron Levie keeps changing his mind about AI productivity, and that's actually the most honest take you'll hear. Meanwhile, the infrastructure companies are betting billions that he's right about one thing: we're going to need a lot more compute to handle all this "productivity."

**Google ships specialized TPUs for the "agentic era"**
Google launched its eighth-generation TPU chips, specifically designed for AI agents. The company is calling this hardware the foundation for "the future of AI" and positioning it as purpose-built for the kind of complex, multi-step AI workflows that agents require.
Why it matters: Google just admitted that current AI chips aren't good enough for what's coming next. If you're running AI agents in production, your infrastructure costs are about to get more predictable but your hardware refresh cycle just got shorter.
https://blog.google/innovation-and-ai/infrastructure-and-cloud/google-cloud/tpus-8t-8i-cloud-next/

**Microsoft's AutoAdapt solves the "why doesn't this AI work for my business?" problem**
Microsoft Research released AutoAdapt, a framework that automatically figures out how to adapt large language models for specific industries like healthcare or legal work. Instead of spending weeks manually choosing between approaches like RAG or fine-tuning, AutoAdapt picks the right strategy and optimizes it within your budget and latency constraints.
Why it matters: Every enterprise AI project starts with "this model doesn't understand our domain." Microsoft is automating the most expensive part of AI deployment. If it works, the consultants charging $200k to customize your AI are about to have a very bad year.
https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/research/blog/autoadapt-automated-domain-adaptation-for-large-language-models/

**Aaron Levie doubles down on Jevons paradox: better AI means more hiring, not less**
Box CEO Aaron Levie expanded on his productivity paradox theory, arguing that as AI makes employees more productive, companies will want more productive employees, not fewer total employees. He's calling this "Jevons paradox" applied to AI, where efficiency gains increase total consumption rather than reducing it.
Why it matters: Your company's "AI will help us do more with less" budget planning is based on the wrong assumption. Levie is predicting the opposite: AI makes work so much faster that you'll take on more projects and hire more people to manage them all.
https://x.com/levie/status/2047747142308274645

**Replit adds one-click imports from Vercel and Lovable**
Replit CEO Amjad Masad announced that developers can now import their apps from Vercel or Lovable to Replit with just a few clicks, making it easier to switch between development platforms.
Why it matters: This is Replit's play to capture developers who build on other platforms but want Replit's AI-powered development experience. One-click migration removes the biggest barrier to switching.
https://x.com/amasad/status/2047747978690232550

**Cursor users swear by GPT-5.5 and Composer 2 combo**
Developer Ryo Lu posted that he's switched entirely to using GPT-5.5 with Composer 2 in Cursor, calling it "the perfect duo for intelligence, speed, and cost." The combination appears to be gaining traction among developers who need powerful coding assistance.
