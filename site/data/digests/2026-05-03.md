**AI Builders Digest — May 3, 2026**

Enterprise AI is having a reality check moment. While Silicon Valley debates AGI timelines, actual companies are focused on fixing the boring stuff that's always been broken. That gap between hype and reality is where the real money gets made.

**Box CEO Aaron Levie: enterprises want AI to fix their actual problems**
Box CEO Aaron Levie shared insights from conversations with enterprises outside Silicon Valley about their AI priorities. These companies aren't chasing futuristic AI visions. They want to speed up customer onboarding, resolve support tickets faster, and ship products quicker. They're looking at slow, inefficient processes and asking if AI can remove the bottlenecks that have constrained them for years.
Why it matters: Every AI startup pitching "revolutionary transformation" is missing the point. Enterprises will pay for AI that makes their existing work 30% faster, not AI that promises to replace everything in five years.

**Google and Kaggle launch 5-day AI agents coding course**
Google brought back its intensive AI agents course with Kaggle, with registration now open. The course focuses on building practical AI agents and is designed for developers who want hands-on experience with agent frameworks and deployment.
Why it matters: Google is training the next wave of developers to think agents-first. When thousands of engineers graduate knowing Google's agent stack better than the alternatives, that's how platform wars get won.

**DeepSeek-V4 Pro lands on Together AI with 512K context**
Together AI added DeepSeek-V4 Pro to its platform with controllable reasoning modes and cached-input pricing specifically for long-context work like code agents and document analysis. The model handles 512K tokens, which is roughly 400 pages of text in a single prompt.
Why it matters: This is the infrastructure play for the "AI that reads everything" use case. When your legal team can upload entire contract databases and get instant analysis, that's not a nice-to-have anymore.
