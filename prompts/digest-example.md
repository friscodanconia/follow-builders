# Example Digest (for style reference — do NOT copy content)

This is what a great issue looks like. Notice the editorial opening has a point of view, the format varies by story significance, and the "Why it matters" lines are sharp and specific.

---

**AI Builders Digest — March 15, 2026**

OpenAI just gave away something that cost them billions to build. And Google's response tells you everything about how this market is about to shift.

**OpenAI makes GPT-5 free for everyone**
The company announced that GPT-5 — its most powerful model — is now free for all ChatGPT users, no subscription required. The paid tier still exists for heavy usage and early access to new features, but the core model that was $20/month last year is now $0. CEO Sam Altman called it "the most important decision we've made since launching ChatGPT."
Why it matters: This is OpenAI's play to lock in users before Google and Anthropic catch up. When the best AI is free, the fight moves to who has the best product around it — and that's a very different competition.
https://openai.com/blog/gpt5-free

**Google cuts Gemini API prices by 80%**
Hours after OpenAI's announcement, Google slashed prices across its Gemini API. The timing is not a coincidence. Gemini Pro now costs roughly the same as running an open-source model on your own servers, which effectively removes the cost advantage of self-hosting.
Why it matters: Developers who spent months setting up their own AI infrastructure are now paying more than they would on Google's cloud. Expect a wave of companies shutting down their self-hosted setups.
https://blog.google/technology/ai/gemini-pricing-update

**Anthropic quietly ships Claude's best coding update yet**
No press release, no blog post — Anthropic just updated Claude's coding abilities in what users on Twitter are calling "the biggest stealth upgrade of the year." Multiple developers reported that Claude can now handle full repository refactors that previously required breaking the work into dozens of small prompts.
Why it matters: While OpenAI and Google fight over pricing, Anthropic is winning on product quality. That strategy has limits — but right now it's working.
https://x.com/developer/status/123456789

**Hugging Face hits 1 million open models**
A milestone for the open-source AI community. Quick context: this was at 500,000 models just eight months ago.
https://huggingface.co/blog/one-million-models

**France's Mistral raises $2B at $15B valuation**
Europe's most valuable AI startup closed another massive round. The company is betting big on enterprise customers who want AI that stays within EU data regulations.
Why it matters: Mistral is proof that "the American labs will win everything" isn't a foregone conclusion — especially when regulation gives local players a structural advantage.
https://mistral.ai/news/series-c

---

Notice:
- The opening connects two stories and has an opinion ("tells you everything about how this market is about to shift")
- The Google story explicitly calls out the timing ("The timing is not a coincidence")
- The Anthropic story has personality ("No press release, no blog post")
- Hugging Face is a quick hit — just two sentences because that's all it needs
- "Why it matters" lines name specific dynamics (pricing wars, self-hosting shutdowns, regulatory advantages) not vague trends
