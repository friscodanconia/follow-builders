**AI Builders Digest — April 9, 2026**

Everyone's talking about Anthropic's Mythos model like it's a breakthrough. The real story is what happens when the people building products with AI start using AI to build faster than everyone else.

**Box CEO Aaron Levie says Mythos proves there's "absolutely no wall" in AI progress**
Aaron Levie called Anthropic's new Mythos model "another clear reminder" that AI capabilities keep climbing with no ceiling in sight. He highlighted meaningful double-digit gains on critical benchmarks and predicted that as coding and tool use improve, so will agentic workflows. Most knowledge automation, he argues, depends on models that can reason through complex tasks and use the right tools with proper context.
Why it matters: When the CEO of a company that handles enterprise data for millions of users says AI capability growth has no limits, he's not just cheerleading. He's planning his product roadmap around the assumption that whatever AI can't do today, it will do next quarter.

**Y Combinator's Garry Tan spots a security loophole in Claude Code**
Garry Tan pointed out an interesting quirk in Anthropic's Claude Code: while the agent can't use its Write File tool outside its designated workspace, it can apparently use "cat >>" bash commands to write to any file in the system. The observation came with a screenshot showing the potential workaround.
Why it matters: This is exactly the kind of edge case that will bite companies hard when they deploy AI agents at scale. Sandbox escapes aren't theoretical problems when the AI is managing your codebase.

**Product manager Peter Yang wonders if Anthropic eats its own dog food**
Peter Yang responded to the Mythos release by asking whether Anthropic has been using the model internally to achieve their "recent insane velocity" of product launches. It's a smart question given how much the company has shipped in recent months.
Why it matters: If Anthropic is using its own AI to build AI faster, that's a compounding advantage no other lab can match until they catch up on model quality.
