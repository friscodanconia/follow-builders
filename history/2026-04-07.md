**AI Builders Digest — April 7, 2026**

Chinese AI company DeepSeek just dropped new models designed specifically for AI agents, while industry leaders are rethinking how AI will actually change work.

**DeepSeek launches AI models built specifically for agents**

Chinese AI company DeepSeek released DeepSeek-V3.2 and DeepSeek-V3.2-Speciale, calling them "reasoning-first models built for agents." These are AI systems designed to power autonomous software that can handle multi-step tasks without constant human guidance. The models focus on improved reasoning capabilities rather than just chat performance.

Why it matters: This signals that the AI agent race is going global, with Chinese companies directly targeting the same autonomous AI assistant market that Silicon Valley is betting on.

**Box CEO pushes back on AI job displacement fears**

Box CEO Aaron Levie argued that AI agents will create more demand for skilled work rather than eliminate jobs. He believes that when AI makes coding easier, companies will apply software to far more parts of their business — building automation for tasks that wouldn't have made economic sense before, like advanced marketing workflows or modernizing legacy systems.

Why it matters: This challenges the prevailing narrative about mass AI job displacement, suggesting we might see job transformation and expansion instead of wholesale replacement.

[https://x.com/levie/status/2040967947275948357](https://x.com/levie/status/2040967947275948357)

**OpenAI launches safety research fellowship program**

OpenAI announced a new Safety Fellowship to fund independent researchers working on AI alignment and safety. The pilot program aims to develop the next generation of safety researchers outside of major tech companies. Few details were provided about funding amounts or selection criteria.

Why it matters: As AI systems become more powerful, having independent voices in safety research becomes crucial for catching risks that company researchers might miss.

**Anthropic secures massive computing power through Google and Broadcom deal**

Anthropic expanded its partnership with Google and Broadcom to access "multiple gigawatts" of next-generation computing infrastructure. This represents a significant scale-up in computational resources for training and running large AI models.

Why it matters: The gigawatt-scale computing commitment shows how much computational power the next generation of AI systems will require — and how expensive the AI arms race is becoming.
