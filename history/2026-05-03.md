**AI Builders Digest — May 3, 2026**

Everyone's figuring out how to work with AI agents, but they're making the same mistake: treating them like really smart interns instead of thinking partners. The difference determines whether you get busywork automation or actual leverage.

**Box CEO Aaron Levie explains the 100x agent economy**
Box CEO Aaron Levie broke down why Atlassian's strong earnings shouldn't surprise anyone. His thesis: when there are 100x more AI agents than humans doing work, the software that powers underlying tasks (code generation, lead processing, contract reviews, invoice handling) will see massive growth. The key insight is identifying which platforms benefit when agents amplify human output versus which get bypassed entirely.
Why it matters: If your company sells software that agents can use to do more work faster, you're about to see hockey stick growth. If you sell software that agents replace entirely, start planning your pivot.

**The "cofounder versus employee" approach to coding agents**
Product leader Zara Zhang shared her philosophy on working with AI coding assistants: treat them as cofounders, not employees. Instead of just giving orders, she presents problems, describes situations, and asks for the agent's opinion on solutions.
Why it matters: The developers who learn to collaborate with AI instead of commanding it will write better code faster. Your junior engineers who figure this out first will outproduce seniors who treat AI like an advanced autocomplete.
