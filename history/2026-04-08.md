**AI Builders Digest — April 8, 2026**

Yesterday's digest covered companies building better AI agents. Today, we're hearing from the people who actually have to manage them — and the reality check isn't pretty.

**Box CEO Aaron Levie: AI agents don't eliminate work, they just move it**
Aaron Levie laid out the uncomfortable truth about AI agents in a detailed thread. When agents handle tasks for you, the work shifts to managing the agents themselves: writing proper instructions, providing context, intervening when they go off track, and reviewing their output. Any weak link in this chain renders the agent's work useless.
Why it matters: Every startup that hired 3 people and gave them AI agents instead of hiring 10 is about to learn this the hard way. The work didn't disappear. It moved to the person who has to babysit the agent.

**Every Media founder Dan Shipper pushes back on "AI kills hierarchies" hype**
Dan Shipper argued that while AI might reduce middle management layers, the idea that organizations won't need hierarchies anymore is "silly." His experience with agents suggests that specialization and hierarchy become more valuable, not less, when AI is involved.
Why it matters: If your company's org chart assumes AI agents replace middle managers, you're going to rehire those managers within 18 months. Someone has to manage the agents.
https://x.com/danshipper/status/2041302485315248595

Anthropic product manager Thariq posted asking for users who unexpectedly ran out of tokens on their MAX 20x plan to screenshare while using Claude Code. The company wants to watch how people actually use the product to improve their usage dashboard.

**Product manager Peter Yang calls out token-based productivity metrics**
Peter Yang compared measuring developer productivity by token usage to measuring it by lines of code written, calling both approaches equally problematic.
Why it matters: Your VP of Engineering is about to start tracking "tokens consumed per developer." That's lines-of-code thinking repackaged for 2026.
