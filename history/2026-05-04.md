**AI Builders Digest — May 4, 2026**

The irony is thick: "prompt" meant the same thing for decades until AI changed everything. Yet as Replit's CEO points out, somehow many things stayed exactly the same.

**Google and Kaggle bring back their AI agents bootcamp**
Google reopened registration for its 5-Day AI Agents Intensive Course with Kaggle, following what appears to have been strong demand from the first round. The course focuses on hands-on agent development rather than theory.
Why it matters: Google is betting that teaching developers to build agents is more valuable than just selling them pre-built ones. If thousands of developers graduate from this program, expect a flood of new agent-based startups by fall.
https://blog.google/innovation-and-ai/technology/developers-tools/kaggle-genai-intensive-course-vibe-coding-june-2026/

**DeepSeek-V4 Pro lands on Together AI with 512K context**
The Chinese lab's latest model is now available through Together AI's API, featuring controllable reasoning modes and cached-input pricing specifically designed for long-context work like code agents and document analysis. Together AI is positioning this as a cost-effective alternative for research synthesis tasks.
Why it matters: DeepSeek keeps proving that the "only American labs can build frontier models" assumption is wrong. More importantly, 512K context with smart caching makes running agents on long codebases actually affordable.
https://www.together.ai/blog/deepseek-v4-pro-now-available-on-together-ai

**Replit CEO Amjad Masad reflects on how "prompt" evolved**
Amjad Masad shared a brief observation about how the word "prompt" took on an entirely new meaning with AI, but noted that many underlying patterns remained unchanged. He didn't elaborate on specifics, but the comment landed with developers who've watched the terminology shift.
Why it matters: The fundamentals of human-computer interaction haven't changed as much as the hype suggests. Good developers were always careful about how they communicated with systems.
https://x.com/amasad/status/2050691458920005737

**Y Combinator CEO Garry Tan criticizes Oakland's tax and spending patterns**
Garry Tan called out Oakland for having the highest per-capita taxes among comparable cities while delivering some of the worst services. He specifically noted that 44% of Measure E revenue goes to pre-approved union raises, criticizing what he sees as dysfunction disguised with moral language.
https://x.com/garrytan/status/2050775806574751816

**Venture investor Nikunj Kothari warns funds are playing it too safe**
Kothari argued that too many venture funds are focused on capping downside rather than maximizing upside, often justifying deals by saying "at least [big company] will acquire them." He suggested this shift toward risk aversion and AUM maximization won't deliver the 5-10x fund returns that LPs expect.
