**AI Builders Digest — April 28, 2026**

Everyone's building AI for the world we have. Sam Altman just suggested we rebuild the world for AI instead.

**Sam Altman wants to redesign your operating system for AI agents**
OpenAI's CEO posted a simple but striking idea: "feels like a good time to seriously rethink how operating systems and user interfaces are designed." He also suggested creating an internet protocol "equally usable by people and agents." The timing isn't random — as AI agents get more capable, they're hitting walls built for human interfaces.
Why it matters: Your laptop's desktop metaphor was designed in 1984 for humans clicking folders. If Altman's right, the next breakthrough isn't better AI models — it's rebuilding the basic software layer so agents and humans can work together naturally.

**Box CEO Aaron Levie spots AI's "job elimination" blind spot**
Levie identified what he calls "AI Gell-Mann amnesia" — people use AI for their own work and see all the manual cleanup required, but assume AI will instantly eliminate other people's jobs. "We all have a much deeper appreciation for the nuances and complexities of the work that we do every day," he wrote, describing how users notice data access issues and context problems in their own workflow but miss them when evaluating other roles.
Why it matters: Your company's "AI will replace half our customer service team" plan probably underestimates the last-mile work that only humans notice. The people doing the job see complexity that managers miss.
https://x.com/levie/status/2048576989930619185

**OpenAI showcases Choco's food distribution AI agents**
The company published a detailed case study on how Choco, a food distribution platform, used OpenAI's APIs to automate ordering and inventory management. The story highlights "real-world AI impact" with specific productivity gains and growth metrics.
Why it matters: These customer stories are OpenAI's proof that enterprise AI works beyond chatbots. Food distribution isn't glamorous, but it's the kind of practical use case that justifies corporate AI budgets.

**Peter Steinberger discovers the power of cloud-based AI testing**
The PSPDFKit founder switched his OpenClaw project's test suite to run on Blacksmith's cloud infrastructure instead of local machines. His reaction: "IT IS SO GOOD. codex can literally spin up to 32vCPU instances and rip through our test suite."
Why it matters: As AI-powered development tools get hungrier for compute, your local machine becomes the bottleneck. Expect more developers to move their AI workflows to the cloud just to keep up with what the tools can do.
