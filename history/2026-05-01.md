**AI Builders Digest — May 1, 2026**

The agent economy is arriving faster than expected. But while everyone's building AI that can spend your money, nobody's talking about the infrastructure breaking under the weight.

**Stripe's data chief reveals what AI agents are actually buying**
Every founder Dan Shipper interviewed Emily Glassberg Sands, who leads data and AI at Stripe, about the emerging agent economy. Stripe processes 2% of global GDP, giving them the clearest view of how AI agents are starting to handle transactions. The conversation covered trust barriers around expensive purchases and what patterns Stripe is seeing in early agent-driven commerce.
Why it matters: When the company that handles transactions for half the internet starts tracking AI purchasing patterns, they're essentially creating the credit score system for the agent economy. The data they're collecting now will determine which agents get trusted with your wallet.

**Claude's response times are getting noticeably slower**
Product manager Peter Yang reported that Claude now regularly takes 3-5 seconds to respond through its basic web interface, with the delays appearing random. Multiple users in the replies confirmed similar experiences across different subscription tiers.
Why it matters: When your AI assistant starts feeling sluggish, you stop using it for quick tasks. If Anthropic can't maintain snappy response times at their current scale, they'll lose users to faster alternatives before they can showcase Claude's superior reasoning.
